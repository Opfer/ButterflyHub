About

What is ButterflyHub?

Browsing through these pages, you will find all kinds of information about North, Central, and South American butterflies. The main focus is on identification of adult butterflies, but you can also learn about immature stages, behaviour, distribution, and seasonality, for example. The information is all contributed, and updated, by butterfly enthusiasts.

Why ButterflyHub?

When I became serious about butterfly photography, I was amazed how little information was available either online or in print publications. But it turns out that there are people out there who know things; the information is just scattered around the globe! I’ve built up a very small network of people who help me - one with Lycaenidae, one with Satyrinae, one with Hesperiidae, etc. and ButterflyHub is where I can share the tips I get from them. The idea is that other people, on their own, or with help from other experts, will also share what they know. Together, we can build an impressive database of butterfly knowledge - much more than I, or anyone else, could do on their own.

How should I use ButterflyHub?

It depends what you want to do, but let’s suppose you have a photograph of a butterfly that you haven’t been able to identify. If you have an idea of what general group the butterfly belongs to (eg. Swallowtail, Hairstreak, Skipper), you could start in the Gallery Page and search for all ButterflyHub photographs from that family, or subfamily. If you find a close match, you can then use the matching butterfly as a Focal Taxon on the Compare Page. There you will find a selection of similar species, and how to identify them from the Focal Taxon. If you believe you have identified your photograph, you can read about it on the Taxon Page, where you can find distribution and other natural history information. Of course, all this depends on how much information has been contributed about certain butterflies. So, finally sign up and upload your photograph - add it to the database!

Can I upload an unidentified photograph?

Yes, of course! You can upload a photograph as a Papilionidae (Swallowtail) without identifying it even to the subfamily, for example. Other users may be able to identity your butterfly for you. Be as specific as you can; upload an unknown grass skipper as a Hesperiinae (grass-skipper subfamily) rather than Heserpiidae (the enormous skipper family which includes the very different spreadwing skippers and others). If you don’t even know the family, we have included “Mysteries” as a place-holder family where your strangest butterflies can start out!

Why only the Americas?

There is no “good” answer for this, it’s just a starting point. The only limitation to expanding to the rest of the world is a list of butterfly taxa from other places. I’ve started with the Americas because that’s where I’ve travelled and photographed butterflies, and because I have a list available. In the future, I do hope to expand ButterflyHub coverage worldwide.
